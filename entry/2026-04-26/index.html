Chef Notes

  • ✦For a lighter version, substitute coconut milk with water or vegetable broth.
  • ✦If using raw chickpeas, soak them overnight and cook until tender.
  • ✦Adjust the amount of dark cacao based on your preference for bitterness or sweetness.

Cooking Steps

  1. 1

    Prep the ingredients: Cook chickpeas until tender (about 45 minutes in a pot with water). Trim asparagus and slice cucumber. Dice onion and mince garlic.

  2. 2

    Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

  3. 3

    Add asparagus and chickpeas to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until asparagus is tender and chickpeas are lightly browned.

  4. 4

    Stir in smoked paprika, cumin, and dark cacao. Cook for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors.

  5. 5

    Pour in coconut milk and stir to combine. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the mixture is slightly thickened.

  6. 6

    Season with sea salt and adjust to taste. Cook until the flavors meld, about 3-4 minutes.

  7. 7

    Serve warm, garnished with cilantro if desired.

Reflection

The recipe emerged from a need to honor slow-cooked richness and cooling clarity. Asparagus and mushrooms were chosen for their earthy texture, while cucumber and coconut milk brought a refreshing contrast. Dark cacao added depth, aligning with the Mayan ceremonial emphasis on transformative ingredients.
