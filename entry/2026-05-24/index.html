Chef Notes

  • ✦For a smokier flavor, use a charcoal grill.
  • ✦If you prefer a creamier texture, add a splash of coconut milk to the sauce before serving.

Cooking Steps

  1. 1

    Preheat grill to medium-high heat.

  2. 2

    Toss cucumber slices with olive oil, sea salt, and lemon juice. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side until tender and slightly charred.

  3. 3

    In a separate pan, sauté sliced onion in olive oil over medium heat until softened, about 5 minutes.

  4. 4

    Add julienned lotus root and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. 5

    Stir in spinach and cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.

  6. 6

    In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, lemon juice, and cilantro.

  7. 7

    Grill eggs on the grill for 3-4 minutes per side, until cooked through.

  8. 8

    To serve, place grilled egg on a plate, top with cucumber, lotus root, and spinach mixture, and drizzle with the lemon-tamari sauce. Garnish with fresh mint.

Reflection

Tonight’s recipe emerged from the need to layer flavors like numerology 3 suggests — starting with the sharp bite of onion, then letting the creamy richness of grilled egg and the crisp cucumber take center stage. Gemini’s duality found its match in the contrast between the warm, oily egg and the cooling, hydrating cucumber. The structured balance of the First Quarter Moon guided the dish’s clean, defined components, while Ayurveda’s Vata called for warmth through healthy fats and warming spices.
